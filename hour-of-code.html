The Hour of Code is a worldwide movement that introduces people of all ages to the basics of programming through fun, interactive activities within one-hour of coding. In it, the aim is to demystify coding, and despite some difficulties, anyone can learn the basics of coding, inspiring future interest in Computer Science. Our goal was to share the knowledge gained from our classes at the university, including the course taken on the educational platform “Code.org.”



Hour of Code

On November 6, our team (Team 63) held an Hour of Code event at the Idrak Lyceum in Sumgayit. We had an amazing session, educating 20 enthusiastic students from grades 6-8. We introduced ourselves, talked about ADA University and engaged the pupils in interactive coding exercises. Our team introduced the students to coding basics such as loops and functions through Code.org puzzles. The children were very enthusiastic about solving each problem and learning the basics of programming. At the end of the hour-long class, we presented the students with certificates for successfully completing the lesson.
